Plants and Coffee

Tuesday, March 31, 2020

When I think Plants and Coffee, I'm instantly reminded of my grandmother. She was one classy lady and she loved her houseplants almost as much as her afternoon coffee. Ritually, before making dinner my grandmother and my mom would sit together at the kitchen table and drink their afternoon coffee. It was with them that I had my first taste of coffee...I was only 3 years old! They indulged me that one time, (but really, they didn't know what hit them, it was a sneak attack and they didn't realize I had gulped down a whole cup until it was too late - mwahahaha) after that it was back to milk with a splash of coffee.

If my grandmother kept her coffee close, she kept her plants even closer...like really close...like in the living room trailing up one wall and wrapped around the perimeter of the ceiling (what can I say, it was the 80's). It was was important to keep the devil's vine (Epipremnum aureum) happy so it could continue taking over the house, and I have no idea where we got this idea but we used left over coffee grinds as plant fertilizer . Does this scientifically work? I have no idea. But I suppose the proof is in the pudding, and that plant grew unencumbered for years and years.

SO don't throw out those grinds, your plants will love you for it!

xoxo Enid

Enid loves writing about coffee about as much as drinking it. She lives in San Francisco with her husband and their dog, Mocha.
